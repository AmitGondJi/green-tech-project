Sustainable Hardware: Buying, Maintaining, and Upgrading Ethically

Manufacturing new electronics accounts for 80% of a device's total lifetime carbon footprint. Making smart purchasing and maintenance decisions is the most powerful action you can take to reduce this impact.

1. Choose Devices with Longevity in Mind

When shopping for new technology, look beyond performance specs and prioritize durability, repairability, and ethical sourcing.

Prioritize Repairability (Right to Repair)

Look for devices that use standard fasteners, swappable batteries, and modular components. Websites like **iFixit** offer repairability scores for most popular electronics.

Look for Certifications

Check for labels like **EPEAT**, **TCO Certified**, and **Blue Angel**. These certifications ensure the product meets high standards for environmental design and supply chain ethics.

2. Maintain and Maximize Life Cycle

A well-maintained computer can easily last 5-10 years. Proper care is key to avoiding premature replacement.

  • Clean Regularly: Dust buildup causes overheating, which reduces component efficiency and lifespan. Clean fans and vents periodically.
  • Strategic Upgrades: A slow computer often only needs a cheap upgrade, like replacing a HDD with an **SSD** or adding more **RAM**, rather than a full system replacement.
  • Software Optimization: Keep your operating system updated and run optimization tools to ensure the device runs efficiently on older hardware.

3. Embrace Refurbished and Second-Hand

Purchasing refurbished devices is an excellent way to acquire quality technology while eliminating the environmental cost of new manufacturing.

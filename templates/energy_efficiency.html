Energy Efficiency: Power-Saving Tips for Your Devices

The IT industry's power consumption is massive. By making small changes to your habits and settings, you can drastically cut down on the energy used by your computer, monitor, and network gear.

1. Smart Use: Tweak Your System Settings

The easiest energy savings come from optimizing how your operating system manages power.

Master Sleep Modes

Set your computer to enter **Sleep or Hibernate mode** after 10-15 minutes of inactivity. **Shut down** completely if you won't use the device for more than 2 hours.

Optimize Display Settings

Use the **lowest comfortable screen brightness**. Also, switch to a **dark theme** or use a simple black desktop background, as this can save energy on modern OLED/AMOLED screens.

  • Close Apps: Shut down background applications and browser tabs you aren't actively using, as they consume CPU and memory, which demands more power.
  • Disable Peripherals: Unplug USB devices, external drives, and even turn off the webcam when not in use.

2. Green Hardware & Physical Habits

Your hardware and how you power it have a massive impact on overall energy consumption.

  1. Combat Vampire Power
    Unplug phone chargers, power adapters, and appliances that continue to draw electricity even when the device is off or fully charged. Use a **power strip** and switch it off completely.
  2. Choose Laptops Over Desktops
    A **laptop** uses significantly less power (often 50-80% less) than a desktop PC performing the same tasks. If performance isn't critical, opt for a laptop or thin client.
  3. Upgrade to Energy Star
    When buying new monitors, printers, or networking gear, look for the **ENERGY STAR** label, which guarantees high energy efficiency standards.

Did you know?

One hour of gaming on a powerful desktop can use as much power as watching **10 hours of video** on a modern laptop.
